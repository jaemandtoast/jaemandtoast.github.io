About Me

First impressions last, which is why I always strive to make mine phenomenal.

I'm currently a third year who has the wonderful privilege of studying Computer Science at UC Berkeley. Even just a year ago, I would have never believed -- or even thought -- that I would end up coding my way to a career; prior to my first Computer Science class at UC Berkeley, I had never written a single line of code. After numerous ventures to explore my passions in an attempt to decide my undergraduate major, I took my very first steps into the Computer Science world. I ultimately continued to pursue it because I realized its powerful potential to contribute to the bigger picture on a global scale. My life aspiration is to utilize my knowledge in Computer Science to help mobilize nonprofit organizations and start my own nonprofit as well someday.

Projects

Here's some cool stuff that I've currently been preoccupied with.

HackingEDU

HackingEDU is a hackathon conceived and executed by students, for students, with the intent of disrupting the classroom and really pushing the envelope on the contemporary education system. This is the largest student run hackathon encompassing all the major San Francisco Bay Area Universities and those along the west coast. I'm currently involved behind-the-scenes as part of the Steering and Marketing Committees, which collaborate to make impactful decisions that affect the event on multiple scales.

DeeMe

DeeMe is a new way to instant message. It lets users combine amazing typography, allowing them to chat using more personalized, beautiful messages that they can hold on to. On its 3rd week since its release, DeeMe currently has 50,000+ downloads and has been featured on ProductHunt and Mashable. I am not the creator of this app, but I have been involved as UC Berkeley's Student Ambassador for this startup in terms of marketing and user-feedback.

AFX Dance

AFX Dance is a hip hop dance group that I have been heavily involved with since my freshman year. This organization has tremendously encouraged my creative growth for the past 3 years and is actually what inspired me to teach myself HTML and CSS because I wanted to make a website for the organization. Although the website itself is very basic at the moment, I plan to make continuous developments to it and aim to launch a fully-functional and sleek version by the time I graduate.
